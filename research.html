Where Do Good Startup Ideas Come From? Kyle Libra · January 23, 2026

The "eureka moment" is a myth. Great startup ideas—Uber, Slack, Airbnb—tend to follow recognizable patterns. This piece breaks down those patterns and offers two practical frameworks for evaluating ideas: one lightweight enough for a side project, one battle-tested at a venture studio.

The Race to Be the New Everything Bank Kyle Libra · November 26, 2025

Fintech platforms and legacy banks are competing for the same prize: becoming the single financial home for consumers and businesses. Tokenization is the weapon, stablecoins are the battlefield, and the stakes are enormous. Here's what builders need to understand about the fight for financial infrastructure.

A Lot of What People Think About Stablecoins Is Wrong Kyle Libra · September 16, 2025

Circle and Tether haven't won—they've commoditized themselves. The real opportunity in stablecoins isn't issuance. It's distribution and infrastructure, and that gap is still wide open for founders who know where to look.

How Defy Owns 17% of Their Best Companies Without Following On Every Round Neil Sequeira, Defy · May 15, 2026

Defy co-founder Neil Sequeira explains how his firm averages 17% ownership across its seven highest-marked companies — competitive with any early-stage manager in the country. He covers why 75% of Defy's deal flow never goes to market, the firm's biggest capital call at the depths of April 2020, and why the most contentious deal at the partner meeting usually performs best. A masterclass in early-stage conviction and portfolio construction.

Fred Wilson on 40 Years in Venture — and Why USV Is Automating Itself Union Square Ventures · April 29, 2026

Fred Wilson walks Union Square with fellow GP Michael Mignano and reflects on four decades in venture: losing his first firm in the dot-com crash, the original USV thesis, the early bets on Twitter and Coinbase, and where the AI "kill zone" leaves startups. The most provocative thread: USV is building AI agents designed to take over work its own partners do today.

Why Product Sense Is the Only Product Skill That Will Matter in the AI Age Shreyas Doshi · March 5, 2026

As AI tools commoditize execution, what separates winning products is the human judgment layered on top. Doshi works backward from that premise to a decomposition of "product sense" — empathy, simulation, strategic thinking, taste, and creative execution — and argues it's the one durable career moat for product people.

The Retail Trade: Crypto vs Equities Wintermute · February 26, 2026

Retail traders are increasingly treating crypto and equities as direct substitutes—not correlated risk-on bets. Wintermute's data-driven analysis explains how institutional ETF adoption is compressing crypto volatility, and what it means for the shape of the next cycle.

From Adoption to Annexation Meltem Demirors · February 24, 2026

Institutional adoption of crypto looks different up close than the headlines suggest. Demirors argues that institutions aren't here to participate in a new economy—they're here to convert it into recurring AUM fees. A sharp counterweight to the uncritical "institutions are coming" narrative.

17 Things We're Excited About for Crypto in 2026 a16z crypto · February 5, 2026

The a16z crypto team's annual outlook: the ideas, technologies, and trends they expect to define the next wave of development. Covers AI-crypto intersections, on-chain governance, and the infrastructure improvements most likely to unlock mainstream adoption.

The AI Revolution Is Here. Will the Economy Survive the Transition? Michael Burry, Dwarkesh Patel, Patrick McKenzie, Jack Clark · January 9, 2026

Four sharp minds—the investor who called 2008, Anthropic's co-founder, and two leading tech thinkers—share a Google Doc and debate AI's economic impact head-on: labor displacement, productivity gains, regulatory responses, and whether the transition will be orderly or not.

Perpetual Futures, Explained Patrick McKenzie · December 5, 2025

Perpetual futures are the dominant trading instrument in crypto—and most people outside the industry have never heard of them. McKenzie explains the mechanics clearly: how funding rates keep perps tethered to spot prices, and why a plurality of all stablecoins exist specifically to collateralize these positions.

Tipping Points: Edelman's Perspective on 2026 Edelman · November 2025

Edelman's annual trends report examines four cultural counter-movements — reactions against globalism, institutions, and the settled truths of the last two decades — that are reshaping how people live, consume, and engage with business. Useful context for anyone building consumer-facing products in a moment of shifting trust.

Crypto Trends Report, 2025 Edition Alana Levin · November 18, 2025

A comprehensive map of the crypto landscape heading into 2025: where capital is flowing, which sectors are gaining momentum, and the macro and regulatory dynamics shaping the market. Essential context for founders and investors deciding where to build next.
